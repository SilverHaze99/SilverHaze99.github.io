Understanding InfoSecFor’s Design

I’m committed to being upfront about how this site works. That includes sharing the design choices behind InfoSecFor and how they might subtly influence you—a concept called "priming." Let’s dive into what’s going on behind the scenes.

Color Palette

I use a dark color scheme with shades of dark gray (#121212, #1e1e1e, #2a2a2a), light gray text (#e0e0e0), and a teal accent (#00bcd4). Here’s why:

  • Dark Backgrounds : Dark colors feel serious and professional, perfect for a site about information security.
  • Light Text : High contrast makes the content easy to read and keeps your focus on the words.
  • Teal Accent : Used for links and buttons, teal suggests trust and a modern, tech-savvy vibe.
  • Gray Footer : Subtle tones keep secondary info from stealing the show.

Note: Color perception varies across cultures. I aim for tones that feel professional and accessible globally.

Typography

I chose 'Segoe UI', a sans-serif font, for its clean, modern look. It’s widely used in tech contexts, making it feel familiar and easy to read while supporting a professional vibe.

Layout and Structure

  • Centered Content: The main content area (max-width: 800px) focuses your attention and improves readability on any screen.
  • Clear Navigation: The header’s dropdown menu organizes info neatly, so you can find what you need fast.
  • Spacious Design: Ample padding and spacing keep the site uncluttered and easy on the eyes.

The Priming Effect of Transparency

Here’s the thing: even sharing these design choices is a form of priming. By being open, I hope to build trust and show my commitment to honesty. But I’m aware this openness might shape how you see the site, making you feel I’m "extra trustworthy." That’s not a trick—it’s just how communication works. I want you to know I’ve thought about it.

Try this: If your browser supports it, toggle between Dark Mode and Light Mode. Notice how the site feels different? That’s priming in action. I’d love to hear your thoughts—share them via GitHub!
